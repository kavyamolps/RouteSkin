Here's a step-by-step guide to determining your skin type at home:

  1. Cleanse Your Face:Start with a clean face. Use a gentle cleanser to remove any makeup, dirt, or oil.

  2. Wait for One Hour:Allow your skin to return to its natural state for about an hour. Avoid touching your face during this time.

  3. Observe Your Skin:After an hour, observe how your skin feels and looks. Pay attention to these characteristics:

    • Dry Skin:If your skin feels tight, flaky, or rough, especially around the cheeks, you likely have dry skin.

    • Oily Skin:If your skin looks shiny or greasy, especially in the T-zone (forehead, nose, and chin), and feels slick to the touch, you likely have oily skin.

    • Combination Skin:If you notice that some areas of your face are dry while others are oily, you likely have combination skin. Typically, the T-zone is oily while the cheeks are dry.

    • Normal Skin:If your skin feels balanced, not too oily or dry, and looks clear and healthy, you likely have normal skin.

  4. Perform a Blotting Test (Optional):If you're unsure about your skin type, you can perform a blotting test. Press a clean tissue against different areas of your face, focusing on the forehead, nose, cheeks, and chin.

    • Oily Skin:If the tissue picks up oil or grease, especially in the T-zone, you likely have oily skin.

    • Dry Skin:If the tissue doesn't pick up much oil, and your skin feels tight or dry afterward, you likely have dry skin.

    • Combination Skin:If some areas of the tissue show oil while others do not, you likely have combination skin.

    • Normal Skin:If there's minimal oil on the tissue and your skin feels comfortable afterward, you likely have normal skin.

  5. Consider Other Factors:Take note of any other skin concerns you may have, such as sensitivity, acne, or aging signs. These can influence your overall skin type and help guide your skincare routine.





To determine if you have sensitive skin, follow these steps:

  1. Patch Test:Choose a small, inconspicuous area of your skin, such as behind your ear or on the inner forearm. Apply a small amount of a new skincare product or ingredient you're testing.

  2. Wait 24 Hours:Leave the product on your skin for 24 hours without washing it off. During this time, avoid applying any other skincare products to the test area.

  3. Observe for Reactions:After 24 hours, observe the test area for any signs of irritation, redness, itching, burning, or discomfort. These reactions may indicate sensitivity to the product or ingredient.

  4. Repeat with Different Products:If you have multiple skincare products you suspect may be causing sensitivity, repeat the patch test process for each product, using a separate test area for each one.

  5. Note Sensations:Pay attention to how your skin feels during and after the patch test. Sensations like stinging, tingling, or tightness can also indicate sensitivity.

  6. Consider Past Experiences:Reflect on any previous instances where your skin reacted negatively to skincare products or environmental factors. If you've experienced sensitivity before, it's likely that you have sensitive skin.

  7. Consult a Dermatologist:If you're unsure or experience persistent skin sensitivity, consult a dermatologist. They can provide a professional assessment and recommend suitable skincare products and routines tailored to your skin's needs.
